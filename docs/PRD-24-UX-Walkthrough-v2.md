# PRD-24 UX Walkthrough v2: What the User Sees

Four scenarios walked through step by step.

---

## Scenario 1: Extracting from a Book

**Setup:** Tenise has "A House United" by Nicholeen Peck uploaded and processed in her Manifest. She wants to extract everything useful from it.

### Step 1: Open the book

She taps "A House United" from the Manifest list. The book detail page shows:
- Title, author, file type icon
- AI summary (2-4 sentences)
- Table of contents (collapsible)
- Tags
- A prominent **Extract** button
- The usual admin actions (Re-process, Archive, Delete)

No usage designation chips. No separate extraction buttons. Clean.

### Step 2: Click Extract

She taps Extract. A genre picker appears — tag-style chips she can tap to toggle:

> **What kind of book is this?** *(select all that apply)*
>
> `Non-Fiction` · `Fiction` · `Biography / Memoir` · `Scriptures / Sacred` · `Workbook` · `Poetry / Essays` · `Allegory / Parable` · `Devotional / Spiritual Memoir`

She taps **Non-Fiction** (it highlights). She could tap multiple if the book blended genres, but this one is straightforward. She taps **Extract**.

### Step 3: Extraction runs

The genre is saved. Three tabs appear below the book info:

**[ Summary | Frameworks | Mast Content ]**

Each tab has its own **progress bar** — a thin bar filling left to right. Summary might finish first, then Frameworks, then Mast Content. She can tap between tabs to watch progress. As each completes, its content replaces the progress bar.

A persistent **Save** button sits in the top-right of the tab area. Next to it, a subtle "Auto-saved ✓" indicator that updates as changes are made.

### Step 4: Browse Summary tab

> **▼ Chapter 1: The Four Basic Skills** *(12 items)*
>
> 🔹 **Key Concept** — Self-government is the ability to control one's own behaviors and emotions so external controls aren't necessary. It's the foundation skill for all family dynamics.
> ❤️ 🗑
>
> 🔹 **Process** — The Four Basic Skills: following instructions, accepting "no" answers and criticism, accepting consequences, and disagreeing appropriately. Each has specific steps children learn.
> ❤️ 🗑
>
> 🔹 **Story** — Peck describes the moment her family hit rock bottom — out-of-control children, marriage under strain — and how self-government principles transformed their household.
> ❤️ 🗑
>
> [ Go Deeper ↓ ]
>
> **▼ Chapter 3: The Rule of Three** *(8 items)*
>
> 🔹 **Key Concept** — The Rule of Three is a structured correction sequence: first instruction, second instruction (pre-teaching the consequence), third instruction (consequence applied). It removes emotion from discipline.
> ❤️ 🗑
>
> ...

She taps ❤️ on the self-government key concept and the Rule of Three. The hearts fill in red. She taps 🗑 on an item about the book's publishing history that isn't useful — it fades out and disappears.

She taps the text of the Rule of Three summary — it becomes an editable field. She adds a personal note: "This is what we tried last Tuesday and it actually worked with [teen]. The key was staying completely calm during step 2." She taps away and it saves.

At the top of the tab, a filter toggle: **[ ❤️ Hearted | All ]**. Currently on "All." She can switch to see just her hearted items.

### Step 5: Browse Frameworks tab

Similar chapter-grouped layout, but each item is a framework principle:

> **▼ Chapter 1: The Four Basic Skills** *(6 principles)*
>
> Self-government is the ability to determine cause and effect, analyze situations, and problem-solve. External rules become unnecessary when a person can govern themselves.
> ❤️ 🗑
>
> The Four Basic Skills form a complete behavioral framework: (1) Following instructions — look at the person, say okay, do the task, check back...
> ❤️ 🗑

She hearts the ones that feel most actionable. Deletes one that's too vague.

### Step 6: Browse Mast Content tab

> **▼ Chapter 1: The Four Basic Skills** *(5 declarations)*
>
> **Self-Government** · *Choosing & Committing*
> "I choose to govern myself first — my reactions, my tone, my follow-through — so my children see self-government modeled before it's expected."

>
> **Structured Boundaries** · *Learning & Striving*

> ❤️ 🗑 [ Send to Mast ]
>
> *Choosing & Committing*
> "I choose to see correction as teaching, not punishment. Every correction is an opportunity to help my child practice a skill they haven't mastered yet."

She taps the text of one declaration — it becomes editable. The AI wrote "I choose to govern myself first" but she wants it more personal: "I choose to govern myself first — especially my tone when I'm tired and the kids are pushing back." She taps away. Saved.

She hearts three that resonate deeply. She taps **"Send to Mast"** on one — a brief confirmation: "Added to your Mast ✓." The button changes to "✓ In Mast."

She doesn't send all of them. The rest stay here for later.

### Step 7: Go Deeper

She's especially interested in Chapter 3 (The Rule of Three). In the Summary tab, she scrolls to that chapter and taps **"Go Deeper ↓"**.

A small progress bar appears within that chapter section. A few seconds later, 3-4 new items appear below the existing ones, marked with a subtle ✦ indicator. These are concepts, stories, and details the first pass didn't capture.

The new items don't duplicate anything — the AI was given existing items as context with instructions to find only additional content.

### Step 8: The Apply Section

Below the three tabs, she sees:

> **Apply This**
>
> [ 💬 Discuss Book ] [ 🎯 Generate Goals ] [ ❓ Generate Questions ] [ ✅ Generate Tasks ] [ 📊 Generate Tracker ]

*(Generate Tracker is slightly greyed with a "Coming Soon" label.)*

### Step 9: Re-run a tab

Later, she realizes she should have also tagged the book as **Workbook** (it has exercises). She taps the genre chips area, adds Workbook, then taps **"Re-run"** on the Summary tab.

> **Re-run Summary extraction?**
> This will replace all summary content for this book with a fresh extraction using the Non-Fiction + Workbook genre lens.
> [ Cancel ] [ Re-run ]

She confirms. Progress bar appears. New summaries arrive, now including exercise descriptions and teaching points per section alongside the conceptual summaries.

## Scenario 2: Single Book Discussion

**Setup:** Tenise has extracted content from "A House United." She wants to ask about a specific concept.

### Step 1: Start from the extraction page

She's browsing the Frameworks tab and sees the Rule of Three principle. She wants to know more — specifically how to set it up with a teenager.

On mobile, she taps the **floating chat button** (💬) in the bottom-right corner of the extraction page.

The **Book Discussion modal** opens. Her question context is pre-loaded — the AI knows she was looking at the Frameworks tab, Chapter 3.

She types: "How would I implement the Rule of Three with a teenager who thinks they're too old for this?"

### Step 2: AI responds with depth

The AI comes in with a substantive answer, drawing from:
- The RAG chunks (actual text from Chapter 3 and other relevant chapters)
- The extracted frameworks (the Rule of Three principle)
- Her personal context (she has teens, Jerrod is her partner)

> The Rule of Three actually works particularly well with teenagers, but Peck emphasizes that the framing has to shift. With younger kids, you're teaching the steps. With teens, you're appealing to their growing sense of autonomy...
>
> The key with a teen who feels "too old" is to reframe it as mutual respect, not compliance. You might try...

### Step 3: Conversation continues naturally

She asks follow-up questions. The AI references specific content from the book, connects it to her family dynamics, suggests practical approaches. This is a real discussion, not a shallow FAQ.

### Step 4: Save and close

The conversation auto-saves. The **"Saved ✓"** indicator is visible. There's also a **Save** button if she wants the reassurance of pressing it.

She taps **"Copy All"** to grab the conversation for later reference, then closes the modal.

The discussion appears in the Discussion Archive on the Manifest list page.

## Scenario 3: Generating Goals from a Book

**Setup:** Tenise has extracted from "Straight Line Leadership" by Dusan Djukich. She wants to turn some principles into actionable goals.

### Step 1: Tap Generate Goals

From the book detail page, she scrolls to the Apply section and taps **"🎯 Generate Goals"**.

The Discussion modal opens. The AI steering is different from a regular discussion:

> I've looked through the frameworks and principles you've extracted from Straight Line Leadership — especially the ones you've hearted. Let me suggest some goals based on what seems to resonate most with you.
>
> **Goal: Shift from "wanting" to "creating" language for one week.**
> Track every time you catch yourself saying "I want to..." and reframe it as "I'm creating..." or "I choose to..." This directly builds the Owner stance Djukich describes.
>
> **Goal: Identify your top 3 circular patterns and define a straight-line alternative for each.**
> Djukich says people revisit the same problems endlessly. Pick three recurring frustrations and write out what the direct A-to-B path looks like for each.
>
> **Goal: Have one "uncomfortable conversation" this week using straight-line principles.**
> Pick something you've been avoiding. Approach it from Owner stance with a clear desired outcome before you start.
>
> Which of these resonate? Want me to adjust any, or explore a different direction?

### Step 2: Conversational refinement

She says: "I love the first two but the third feels too vague. Can you make it more specific to my situation with [teen's name] about homework?"

The AI refines, knowing her family context. They go back and forth shaping the goals until she's happy.

### Step 3: Send to Rigging

When she's satisfied, she taps **"Send to Rigging"** at the bottom of the modal. The goals are auto-formatted as Rigging items and routed. A confirmation appears:

> 3 goals sent to Rigging ✓

The entire conversation (including the refinement) stays in the Book Discussion archive.

## Scenario 4: Multi-Book Discussion

**Setup:** Tenise has extracted from "A House United," "Straight Line Leadership," and "The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People." She wants to explore how they connect.

### Step 1: Open multi-book discussion

From the Manifest list page, she taps **"Discuss Books"** in the header.

A book selector opens:

> **Select books to discuss**
>
> ☑ A House United — Nicholeen Peck
> ☑ Straight Line Leadership — Dusan Djukich
> ☑ The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People — Stephen Covey
> ☐ Boundaries — Henry Cloud & John Townsend
> ☐ The Screwtape Letters — C.S. Lewis
>
> **3 books selected**
>
> Audience: [ Personal ▾ ]
>
> [ Start Discussion ]

She checks three books and taps **Start Discussion**.

### Step 2: AI opens with cross-book synthesis

> You've picked three books that look different on the surface — parenting methodology, coaching philosophy, personal effectiveness. But there's a thread running through all three.
>
> All three are fundamentally about self-government before influence. Peck says you can't teach children self-government until you model it. Djukich says you can't lead from a straight line until you stop living in circles. Covey calls it "Private Victory precedes Public Victory" — Habits 1-3 before 4-6.
>
> Here are some questions at the intersection — pick one, or take this wherever you'd like:
>
> 🔹 Peck's "calm authority" and Djukich's "Owner stance" and Covey's "proactive" — are these the same thing in different clothes? Or are there meaningful differences in what each is asking of you?
>
> 🔹 All three would say transformation starts internally, not with techniques. But you have a household to run TODAY. Where's the tension between "become the person" and "just follow the steps"?
>
> 🔹 You've hearted declarations from at least two of these books. How do your declarations about calm leadership and your framework principles about Owner vs. Victim interact in real life? What happens when "being calm" feels like "being a victim of your child's behavior"?

### Step 3: Deep conversation

Tenise picks a question and they're off. The AI weaves between all three books, pulling from RAG chunks, extracted content, her hearted items, and her personal context. It might:

- Connect Covey's "Begin with the End in Mind" to Peck's approach to parenting goals
- Link Djukich's "creating vs. wanting" to how she handles her children's behavior change
- Reference her own Mast declarations to make connections personal

### Step 4: Switch to Family audience

She realizes this would make an amazing family discussion. She changes the audience selector to **Family**.

The AI adjusts:

> Great idea. Here are questions you could bring to the dinner table:
>
> **For your teens:** "If self-government means controlling your own behavior so nobody else has to — what's one area where you already do that well? What's one area where it's hard?"
>
> **For the whole family:** "We talk about following instructions and accepting consequences. But Covey says the most important habit is being proactive — choosing your response instead of reacting. Can you think of a time this week when you were proactive vs. reactive?"
>
> **Discussion starter:** "All three of these books say the same thing differently: change yourself before trying to change your situation. Do you agree? When has trying to change the situation first actually made things worse?"

### Step 5: Generate Discussion Questions

She wants to save these family questions. She taps **"Send to Lists"** — the questions are formatted as a list and routed to Lists.

> Discussion questions saved to Lists ✓

### Step 6: Archive

She closes the modal. The full conversation (personal reflection + family questions) is saved in the Discussion Archive.

## What's NOT in These Flows

- **No Keel extraction anywhere on the Manifest page.** Keel lives on its own page.
- **No usage designation toggles.** No General Reference, Store Only, etc. Every book is just a book.
- **No RAG search at the Helm.** The Helm gets book knowledge through the settings-controlled extracted/hearted content, not through similarity search.
- **No bulk "Send All to Mast."** Individual declarations only — intentional curation.
- **No re-uploading.** Existing books keep their RAG chunks. New extraction runs on top.
- **No interrogation in single-book discussions.** The AI acknowledges context and lets the user lead.
- **No "I am [finished result]" declarations.** Every declaration passes the honesty test.
